Interactive Experience Research Project

Looking at: Luni.app

1. What was the first thing you paied attention to?

- Luni's site brought my attention with their use a overly nicely animated homepage which immediately reacted to my mouse movements

2. What did you do?

Interaction with the site was mostly out of curiosity, clicking random objects at times, seeing if there were responses to my actions. I ended up trying scrolling and more clicking, then looked into the site’s statements and saw how they are animated and reactive to my actions.

3. What did you spend the most time on?

I spent most of the time exploring around the site. I ended up spending time looking into what the company has to offer.

4. What was the most common action during the experience?

Clicking around is a common action when exploring the number of interactive points the experience has to offer. There are small physical objects that can be clicked, turning them into a game

5. What did you think the intention or goal of the experience was?

The amount of time you spend exploring is done with intention, to keep you hooked and looking at everything the company has to offer. It allows users to explore normally ignored pages by gamifying them, leading them to where they want to be.

6. How does the site achieve its goal?

Continuous scrolling that takes you to the next section of the site naturally lets you experience the brand’s identity and explore each branch they have.

7. What was your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

From the looks of it, the experience is supposed to be fun and unique, but that feeling quickly dies out fairly quickly, as a first-time visit is already enough to visit most of the whole site. It should be at least 1 or 2 times a user would normally visit that site.

8. How should the site be interacted?

The interaction is very intuitive as the immediate reaction surrounding the moment creates an indication that something can be interacted with, as well as hover events.

9. What other media does it reference?

Luni seems to show some familiarity by using some common design tropes in web design, such as having prompts (or tab names) when hovering over an object

10. How should these references communicate to you on how you act?

The site uses familiarity to not make a unique experience alienating, common design tropes are there as the site assumes that a user can navigate a basic digital environment. It allows you to explore the site more freely.

11. How should these references make you feel

The references make the design feel less scary to use and let you be more in awe of the site’s uniqueness. It’s inviting yet unique.

Lumi’s overall site design is calming and well animated; everything feels flowy, which makes it more satisfying to use. It contrasts with the normal sites people use every day, with harsh transitions and less reaction feedback.
